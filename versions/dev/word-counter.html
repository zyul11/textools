Free Online Word Counter — Accurate, Fast, Private

Our free word counter helps writers, students, SEO professionals, and translators count words, characters, sentences, paragraphs, and reading time instantly. Unlike many other word counting tools, Textools Word Counter runs entirely in your browser — your text never leaves your device, and nothing is uploaded to any server. The tool provides real-time statistics as you type or paste text. You get instant feedback on word count, character count (with and without spaces), number of sentences, paragraph count, and estimated reading time. This makes it an indispensable companion for anyone working with text — from drafting social media posts that fit platform limits to ensuring academic essays meet strict word count requirements.

Our word counter supports all Unicode text, including CJK characters (Chinese, Japanese, Korean), accented Latin characters, Cyrillic, Arabic, and emoji. Whether you are writing in English, Chinese, Spanish, French, German, or any other language, the tool accurately counts every character and word. The word counting algorithm splits text on whitespace, which works reliably for space-delimited languages while correctly handling non-space-delimited scripts by counting characters individually.

Why Use a Word Counter Every Day?

Word count awareness is critical in almost every field of writing and communication. Journalists need to hit precise article lengths. Students must stay within essay word limits or risk point deductions. SEO specialists optimize meta descriptions to stay under 160 characters for proper Google search result display. Social media managers craft tweets within 280 characters and Instagram captions within 2200 characters. Translators often bill by word count, making accurate counting essential for fair pricing.

Beyond raw numbers, our word counter helps you understand the readability and structure of your text. Reading time estimates guide content creators in matching reader attention spans — blog posts of 5-7 minutes (1000-1400 words) perform best for engagement, while product descriptions thrive at 100-200 words. The sentence and paragraph counts help identify overly long sentences or dense blocks that may confuse readers.
