This Agriculture Machinery Website Scored 25/100 on SEO — Here's Every Mistake It Made

📅 May 21, 2026 · Case Study SEO Mistakes Agriculture

We ran a free SEO audit on an agriculture machinery website. The result was a brutal wake-up call:

Score: 25 / 100
9 of 22 checks passed · 15 issues found
6 critical · 3 major · 6 informational

The owner of this site sells tractors, harvesters, and farm equipment — products that farmers search for every day. But the site is effectively invisible to Google. Here's the full breakdown of what went wrong, and how any site in a similar situation can fix it.

🔴 Critical: No SSL / HTTPS (Score Impact: Severe)

The single biggest issue. This site runs on HTTP only — no SSL certificate, no HTTPS. In 2026, this is the SEO equivalent of showing up to a business meeting in your pajamas.

Why it matters: Google publicly confirmed HTTPS as a ranking signal back in 2014. Every modern browser marks HTTP sites as "Not Secure" with a visible warning. For a commerce site selling machinery, visitors seeing that warning will bounce immediately. That's lost traffic, lost trust, and lost sales — all because of a free SSL certificate that takes 15 minutes to set up.

🔴 Critical: No H1 Tag (Score Impact: Severe)

Not a single <h1> tag was found on the homepage. The H1 is the most important HTML heading — it tells search engines and users exactly what the page is about. Without it, Google has to guess your page's primary topic.

Why it matters: Imagine writing a book without a title. That's what your homepage looks like to Google without an H1. If you're selling combine harvesters and your page has no H1, you're not going to rank for "combine harvester for sale" — period.

🔴 Critical: No Structured Data (JSON-LD) (Score Impact: Severe)

Zero JSON-LD structured data was found. No Product schema, no Organization schema, no breadcrumb markup. Search engines have no machine-readable way to understand what this business sells.

Why it matters: Structured data powers rich results in Google — product price snippets, star ratings, FAQ accordions, breadcrumb trails. For an e-commerce site selling physical products, missing structured data means missing out on the visual enhancements that make listings stand out (and get clicked) in search results.

🔴 Critical: No OG / Twitter Meta Tags (Score Impact: Severe)

When someone shares this site on social media — Facebook, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, WhatsApp — the link renders as a bare URL with no image, no title, no description. It looks broken.

Why it matters: Social shares are a significant traffic source for most businesses. Without Open Graph and Twitter Card tags, every single social share of this site is a wasted opportunity. Farmers sharing a product link in a WhatsApp group will see nothing but a dead URL.

🔴 Critical: No Sitemap / Robots.txt (Score Impact: Severe)

The sitemap and robots.txt files returned errors. Google's crawlers can't efficiently discover and index the site's pages. Without a sitemap, some pages may never get indexed at all.

Why it matters: A sitemap is literally a map you give to Google saying "here are all my pages, please index them." Without it, Google has to discover pages by following links — and on a small site with limited internal linking, that means many pages stay in indexing purgatory forever.

🔴 Critical: No Canonical URL (Score Impact: Severe)

No canonical tag was set. If this site's content ever gets scraped or republished elsewhere (or if the site is accessible via multiple URLs like with and without www), Google can't determine which version is the original.

Why it matters: Without a canonical tag, duplicate content issues can tank your rankings. Google might split ranking signals across multiple versions of the same page, or worse — index a scraper site as the original.

The bottom line on these 6 critical issues: They're all fixable. Every single one. HTTPS costs $0 (Let's Encrypt is free). Adding an H1 takes 10 seconds. Structured data can be generated with a schema.org template. But leaving them unfixed makes your site invisible to search engines and untrustworthy to visitors.

⚠️ Major Issues

12 of 24 Images Missing Alt Text

Half the product images on this site have no alt text. For an agriculture machinery store, product images are crucial — farmers want to see the equipment before inquiring. Without alt text, Google Image Search can't surface these images, and visually impaired users get no context.

The fix: Go through every product image and add descriptive alt text. Not "image001.jpg" — but "John Deere-style tractor-mounted corn harvester in field, side view." Every product image is a potential entry point via Google Images.

Only 425 Words of Content

The homepage contains just 425 words. For a site selling heavy machinery — where buyers do extensive research before purchasing — this is far too little content to rank for competitive keywords.

The fix: Add detailed product descriptions, an about page with company history and factory photos, and a blog covering farming tips, machinery maintenance, and buying guides. Each page should aim for 800-1500 words of useful content.

🟡 Informational Issues

  • No Google Analytics / GA4 — Cannot track visitor behavior, traffic sources, or conversions
  • Missing meta descriptions on some pages — Google will auto-generate snippets, often poorly
  • No internal links to product pages from homepage — Each product page is an orphan, hard for Google to discover
  • Missing favicon — Small branding miss, but visible in every browser tab
  • Content-Type header could be optimized — Minor technical cleanup
  • HTML lang attribute not explicit — Helps search engines understand the page language
